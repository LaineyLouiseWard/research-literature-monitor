# Research Scope

Compact context for automated paper screening. Keep this file short to minimise token cost.

---

## Core question

How does subseasonal-to-seasonal (S2S) forecast skill vary for multi-hazard events — especially drought-flood transitions — and under which large-scale atmospheric states does this skill emerge?

## Thesis framing

Flood-drought transitions are treated as temporally compounding multi-hazard events. The work bridges two literatures that remain weakly connected: (1) S2S prediction/predictability and (2) compound/consecutive hydroclimatic extremes (drought-flood abrupt alternation, precipitation whiplash). Event definitions are aligned to S2S forecast windows rather than instantaneous threshold crossings. Forecast skill is assessed for events, not just individual variables, and then conditioned on large-scale atmospheric state.

## Applied work

C3S seasonal reforecasts (ECMWF SEAS5.1) verified against ERA5-Land and Irish station observations. Deterministic verification (RMSE, Bias, ACC) at weekly lead-time resolution (wk1-wk9). Point-based, Ireland.

## Include — topics that are relevant

- S2S / subseasonal / extended-range prediction and predictability
- Compound, consecutive, or sequential hydroclimatic extremes
- Drought-flood transitions, drought-flood abrupt alternation (DFAA), hydrological whiplash, precipitation whiplash
- Forecast verification for S2S timescales (probabilistic or deterministic)
- Verification of extreme or compound events specifically
- Sources of S2S predictability (MJO, ENSO, NAO, IOD, stratospheric events, land-atmosphere coupling)
- Teleconnection influence on multi-hazard occurrence or predictability
- Windows of opportunity / forecasts of opportunity at subseasonal timescales
- Machine learning applied to S2S forecasting or forecast postprocessing
- Explainable AI (XAI) applied to climate/weather prediction or geoscience
- Hazard indices for drought, flood, or transitions (SPI, SPEI, SRI, SWAP, MSDFI, DFAA indices)
- Multi-model ensembling for S2S
- Event-based predictability assessment

## Include — methods of interest

- Probabilistic and deterministic forecast verification (CRPS, Brier, proper scoring rules, reliability)
- Neural networks, random forests, or hybrid ML-dynamical methods for subseasonal prediction
- XAI methods (SHAP, saliency, LRP) applied to geoscience problems
- Extreme-value theory, copula methods for compound events
- Run theory or window-based event identification

## Exclude — topics to ignore

- Pure climate projection studies (CMIP, SSP scenarios) with no forecasting component
- Numerical weather prediction at synoptic scales (days 1-5) unless explicitly linked to S2S
- Ocean-only or sea-ice-only prediction with no atmospheric/hydrological relevance
- Pure remote sensing or satellite retrieval methodology
- Agricultural yield modelling unless directly linked to S2S forecast skill
- Air quality, pollution, or urban heat island studies
- Palaeoclimate reconstructions
- Pure statistical theory with no geoscience application
- Semantic segmentation, computer vision (unrelated to weather/climate)
- Energy systems or market forecasting unless evaluating S2S forecast inputs

## Geographic relevance

All regions are relevant — the thesis draws on global S2S literature. No geographic filter should be applied.

## Timescale filter

Papers must involve subseasonal (weeks 1-6), seasonal (1-6 months), or extended-range (10-30 day) timescales. Exclude papers focused exclusively on weather (days 1-5) or climate (decadal+) timescales.
